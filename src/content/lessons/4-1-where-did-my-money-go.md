## The situation

You get to the end of the month and the money's gone, but if someone asked you to explain where, you'd have to shrug. Not because you did anything reckless — there was no single big purchase. It just slipped out in pieces, and the pieces are invisible until you go looking for them.

## The idea

Here's the rule that the whole rest of budgeting sits on: **you can't manage money you can't see.** Before any system, any split, any app — you need an honest picture of where your money actually goes right now. Not where you *think* it goes. Where it really goes.

The good news: getting that picture is easy and quick. You don't need a spreadsheet, a fancy app, or a finance degree. You need one week.

For seven days, write down every dollar that leaves your hands. Coffee, gas, rent, that impulse phone charger, the subscription that renewed — all of it. Use your phone's notes app, snap photos of receipts, or jot it on paper. The tool doesn't matter. The *seeing* matters.

And to be clear: **this is not about guilt.** You're not grading yourself. You're a detective gathering clues, not a judge handing out sentences. Some of what you find will surprise you, and that surprise is exactly the point — it's a leak you didn't know you had.

## By the numbers

Back in lesson 1.3 we met the quiet leak: a $25 want you barely remember, three times a week. That's $75 a week, about **$3,900 a year** — roughly a starter emergency fund, flowing out $25 at a time without a real decision ever being made.

The thing is, you'd never notice that $3,900 by feel. It never shows up as one scary number. It only shows up when you write the small numbers down and add them up. That's the whole magic of tracking: it turns a hundred invisible $25s into one visible $3,900 you can actually do something about.

You're not committing to a forever-system yet. Later lessons give you the easy ones — a simple split (4.2), paying yourself first (4.3), automation that does the work for you (4.4). This week is just the snapshot. Look first; decide later.
